Frequently Asked Questions

RBF structure, Angor settlement, and why we replaced the earlier SAFE & token plan

Wasn't a SAFE originally promised?

Yes—early communication referenced a future SAFE. We replaced it with a Revenue-Based Financing structure so Nostria can remain a distributed, non‑equity organization and avoid unnecessary jurisdictional securities overhead. RBF lets contributors share upside without governance dilution.

Are there tokens or NFTs?

No. There is no token issuance in the live structure. All allocation is ledgered via Angor contributions + weighting logic. This reduces regulatory ambiguity and eliminates market speculation distractions.

How is my share calculated?

Your Percentage Share = (Weighted Contribution) / (Total Weighted Capital). Pre-Seed amounts multiply by 1.15. Distribution each month = Percentage Share × (50% of Net Revenue).

Why use Angor?

Angor provides structured contribution recording and automated/assisted BTC distribution, reducing operational friction and avoiding bespoke token contracts.

How long do distributions last?

Indefinite—distributions continue while premium revenue exists. They only stop if the product is discontinued, regulators require cessation, operations become non-viable, or all parties formally agree to restructure. Hitting a 4× cumulative payout simply triggers a review; it does not auto‑end or cap your participation. Any earned but unpaid amounts are still processed.

What happens after a 4× payout?

Reaching 4× cumulative distributions initiates an optional review window. Possible paths: (1) continue status quo; (2) mutually adjust investor pool percentage going forward; (3) change to a different structured agreement.

Can I transfer my rights?

Rights are non-transferable without written approval to prevent informal secondary markets that could trigger securities treatment.

What about taxes?

You should treat distributions as income in your jurisdiction (consult professional advisors). We provide monthly statements & BTC transaction references for record-keeping.

How is this different from equity / SAFE?

No dilution, no cap table, no governance—only proportional access to 50% of ongoing Net Revenue. The earlier SAFE concept was deprecated to maintain decentralisation and simplicity.
